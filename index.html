How Personality Theories See Me

Psychodynamic Theory

Ego: Helps balance my academic ambitions with relaxation.

Defense Mechanisms: I use rationalization when facing failure.

Unconscious Thoughts: I seek validation due to past experiences.

Key Figure: Sigmund Freud

Humanistic Theory

Self-Concept: Hardworking but struggles with self-doubt.

Ideal vs. Real Self: Motivates me to grow.

Unconditional Positive Regard: I value accepting relationships.

Key Figure: Carl Rogers

Trait Theory

Openness: High, as I love learning.

Conscientiousness: Strong, keeping me disciplined.

Neuroticism: Moderate, as I manage stress.

Key Figure: Gordon Allport

Social-Cognitive Theory

Reciprocal Determinism: My habits influence my motivation.

Locus of Control: Internal, believing effort leads to success.

Self-Efficacy: High, as past successes drive me.

Key Figure: Albert Bandura

Conclusion: While each theory offers a different view of my personality, understanding them helps me reflect, grow, and adapt in life.
